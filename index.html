Clinical Workbench

Assessment, screening, teaching, and documentation tools.

Clinical Assessment

Mental Status Exam

Documents appearance, behavior, speech, mood, thought process, cognition, insight, judgment, and other mental-status findings. Produces a narrative or list-style note.

Quick Risk Assessment

Brief clinical review of suicide and self-harm risk, protective factors, acute and chronic risk levels, interventions, and disposition.

Comprehensive Suicide Assessment

Detailed assessment of suicidal ideation, behavior, intensity, access to means, risk and protective factors, formulation, and recommendations. Includes a generated C-SSRS classification when supported by the responses.

Brief Screening

PHQ-9

Nine-item measure of depressive symptoms and related functional difficulty. Includes adolescent timeframe guidance and review of the self-harm item.

GAD-7

Seven-item measure of generalized anxiety symptoms over the past two weeks, with a functional-difficulty item and adolescent-use guidance.

MDQ

Reviews lifetime manic or hypomanic symptoms, whether symptoms occurred together, and the degree of associated impairment.

ASRS v1.1

Six-item adult ADHD screener covering attention, organization, restlessness, and impulsivity during the past six months.

PC-PTSD-5

Five-item adult PTSD screener following a trauma-exposure question. Covers intrusion, avoidance, hypervigilance, detachment, and guilt or blame.

CRAFFT 2.1

Substance-use and associated-risk screening for ages 12–21, including riding in or driving a vehicle while impaired.

Adverse Childhood Experiences (ACEs)

Records exposure to ten categories of childhood abuse, neglect, and household adversity. The score reflects exposure count, not symptom severity.

Benevolent Childhood Experiences (BCEs)

Records ten positive childhood experiences involving safety, dependable care, supportive relationships, routines, and positive self-regard.

WHO-5

Five-item measure of positive mental well-being over the past two weeks, scored as a raw total and percentage.

In-Depth Measures

ISAS

Records lifetime nonsuicidal self-injury behaviors and scores 13 possible functions of self-injury across interpersonal and intrapersonal domains.

Y-BOCS/CY-BOCS

Documents obsessive-compulsive symptom patterns and rates severity through time, interference, distress, resistance, and control. Includes adult and child/adolescent versions.

Trauma Symptoms of Discrimination Scale (TSDS)

Measures trauma-related symptoms associated with discriminatory experiences and records the attributed sources of discrimination.

Level of Personality Functioning Scale (LPFS-SR)

Adult self-report measure of identity, self-direction, empathy, and intimacy, with domain and overall personality-functioning scores.

Functional Assessment of Self-Mutilation (FASM)

Records self-harm methods, frequency, timing, context, and reasons, with scores for four functional reinforcement patterns.

RCADS-47

Forty-seven-item youth measure covering separation anxiety, generalized anxiety, panic, social anxiety, obsessive-compulsive symptoms, and depression. Reports raw subscale scores.

DERS

Thirty-six-item measure of emotion-regulation difficulties across nonacceptance, goals, impulse control, awareness, strategies, and emotional clarity.

CAT-Q

Adult self-report measure of autistic camouflaging across compensation, masking, and assimilation. It does not establish an autism diagnosis.

CATS

Youth trauma measure for ages 7–17 covering potentially traumatic events, PTSD symptoms, and interference with daily functioning.

PCL-5

Twenty-item adult measure of DSM-5 PTSD symptoms during the past month, with total and symptom-cluster scores.
