FASM

Functional Assessment of Self-Mutilation

Document methods, frequency, medical treatment, contextual features, and functions of deliberate self-harm during the past year.

The measure’s original title is retained. Current clinical terminology generally uses nonsuicidal self-injury (NSSI) when the behavior occurs without intent to die.

No diagnostic cutoff is established. Function scores are mean item ratings from 0-3, allowing comparison across subscales of different lengths. They describe reported reasons and should be integrated with interview, antecedents, consequences, context, and safety assessment.

Clinical Note

The FASM is a functional self-report assessment, not a diagnosis or a substitute for direct suicide risk assessment. The four function profiles reflect the reinforcement model studied in adolescent samples; the client’s own explanation and clinical context remain central.

Measure: Lloyd, Kelley, and Hope (1997). Four-function scoring: Nock and Prinstein (2004, 2005).
